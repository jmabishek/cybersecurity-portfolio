DAY - 1 REFLECTIONS

I really learned a lot today.

Going in, I thought I'd finish Level 1, 2, and 3
of my roadmap in one shot. Surprisingly, I only
got through Level 1 - which is just two commands,
ls and pwd. But that's okay, because I didn't
just learn the commands. I learned WHY they exist
and HOW they actually work.

I used to think pwd was just a tool that shows
location. I didn't know there was more to it -
that it has limits, that it can't take arguments,
that it's the first thing professionals run before
doing anything. Same with ls - I thought it just
listed files. I had no idea it could also show
permissions, file types, owners, sizes. That whole
"drwx------" output was new territory for me.

The biggest surprise was history. Even when you
clear the screen, history stays. Every command
you've typed is recorded. That changed how I see
the terminal - it's not just a place where I
type things, it's a place that REMEMBERS what I
typed. I now understand why attackers try to wipe
history, and why investigators dig through it to
catch them. Defenders compare history with their
logs to reconstruct what happened. That blew my
mind a little.

Flags also clicked today. Before, I thought ls
was one command. Now I see ls, ls -a, ls -l,
ls -la as different powers of the same command.
Flags are like attachments - they give a command
new abilities. -a unlocks hidden files, -l unlocks
permissions, -R unlocks recursion. Same command,
different superpowers.

What I'm taking from Day 1 - it's not about
finishing fast. It's about understanding deeply.
I learned more in two commands than I thought
I would in ten. And I'm thankful for that.

Excited for Day 2.
